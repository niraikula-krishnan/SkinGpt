DISEASE: Warts
ALSO KNOWN AS: Verruca, HPV Warts, Common Warts, Plantar Warts

OVERVIEW:
Warts are noncancerous skin growths caused by the human papillomavirus (HPV). They are very common especially in children and spread through direct contact. Most warts resolve on their own but treatment speeds resolution.

CAUSES:
- Human papillomavirus (HPV) infection of the outer skin layer
- Spreads through direct skin-to-skin contact
- Spreads via contaminated surfaces like floors and towels
- Cuts and breaks in skin allow virus entry
- Weakened immune system increases susceptibility

SYMPTOMS:
- Common warts: rough raised bumps, usually on hands and fingers
- Plantar warts: hard, flat growths on soles of feet, may be painful when walking
- Flat warts: smooth, flat-topped, usually on face and legs
- Filiform warts: thread-like projections on face around mouth and nose
- Periungual warts: rough bumps under or around nails
- Small black dots (clotted blood vessels) visible inside warts

TREATMENT:
- Salicylic acid: most common OTC treatment, applied daily for weeks
- Cryotherapy: liquid nitrogen freezing by a doctor
- Electrosurgery and curettage: burning and scraping
- Laser treatment for stubborn warts
- Immunotherapy: imiquimod cream to stimulate immune response
- Many warts resolve without treatment in 1-2 years

IS IT CONTAGIOUS: Yes — warts spread through direct contact and contaminated surfaces.

WHEN TO SEE A DOCTOR:
- Wart is painful, bleeding, or rapidly multiplying
- Warts on face or genitals
- Wart not responding to 3 months of OTC treatment
- Uncertain if growth is a wart or something else

PREVENTION:
- Avoid touching other people's warts
- Do not share towels, razors, or shoes
- Wear flip-flops in public showers, pools, and locker rooms
- Keep hands clean and cuts covered
- HPV vaccine protects against certain strains
