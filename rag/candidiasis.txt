DISEASE: Candidiasis
ALSO KNOWN AS: Candida, Yeast Infection, Thrush

OVERVIEW:
Candidiasis is a fungal infection caused by Candida yeast, most commonly Candida albicans. It affects moist areas of the skin, mouth, and genitals.

CAUSES:
- Overgrowth of Candida yeast naturally present on skin
- Antibiotics disrupt normal bacterial balance allowing yeast to overgrow
- Weakened immune system from diabetes, HIV, or chemotherapy
- Obesity creates warm moist skin folds
- Poor hygiene, wet clothing, tight-fitting clothes
- Pregnancy hormonal changes

SYMPTOMS:
- Red, itchy rash with satellite pustules around edges
- Affects skin folds: armpits, groin, under breasts, between fingers
- White cottage-cheese like discharge (vaginal)
- White patches in mouth and throat (oral thrush)
- Cracking or soreness at corners of mouth (angular cheilitis)
- Nail infections causing thickened discolored nails

TREATMENT:
- Antifungal creams: clotrimazole, miconazole for skin
- Oral antifungals: fluconazole for severe or internal infections
- Nystatin for oral thrush
- Keep affected areas clean and dry

IS IT CONTAGIOUS: Mildly — can spread through direct contact but not easily between healthy people.

WHEN TO SEE A DOCTOR:
- Rash not improving with over-the-counter antifungals after 1-2 weeks
- Recurrent infections
- Widespread rash or fever

PREVENTION:
- Keep skin clean and dry especially skin folds
- Wear breathable cotton clothing
- Avoid prolonged antibiotic use when possible
- Control blood sugar in diabetics
