DISEASE: Acne
ALSO KNOWN AS: Acne Vulgaris

OVERVIEW:
Acne is a chronic inflammatory skin condition where hair follicles become clogged with oil and dead skin cells. It is the most common skin condition worldwide, primarily affecting teenagers but also adults.

CAUSES:
- Excess sebum production by sebaceous glands
- Clogging of hair follicles with dead skin cells
- Bacterial growth, particularly Cutibacterium acnes
- Hormonal changes during puberty, menstruation, pregnancy
- Medications such as corticosteroids and androgens
- Diet high in refined carbohydrates and dairy
- Stress increases cortisol which worsens acne
- Genetics and family history

SYMPTOMS:
- Whiteheads (closed clogged pores)
- Blackheads (open clogged pores)
- Red tender bumps called papules
- Pimples or pustules with pus at tips
- Large painful nodules under skin
- Painful cystic lesions under skin
- Appears on face, forehead, chest, upper back, shoulders

TYPES:
- Mild acne: mostly whiteheads and blackheads
- Moderate acne: papules and pustules
- Severe acne: nodules and cysts, prone to scarring

TREATMENT:
- Topical retinoids such as tretinoin and adapalene to unclog pores
- Topical antibiotics like clindamycin to kill bacteria
- Benzoyl peroxide kills bacteria and reduces oil
- Salicylic acid exfoliates and unclogs pores
- Oral antibiotics like doxycycline for moderate to severe acne
- Oral isotretinoin for severe cystic acne
- Hormonal therapy for women
- Chemical peels and laser therapy for scarring

IS IT CONTAGIOUS: No, acne is not contagious.

COMPLICATIONS:
- Permanent scarring
- Dark spots from post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation
- Low self-esteem and depression

WHEN TO SEE A DOCTOR:
- Over-the-counter treatment does not work after 2 to 3 months
- Acne is severe, painful, or cystic
- Acne causes emotional distress or scarring
- Sudden severe adult acne may indicate a hormonal issue

PREVENTION:
- Wash face twice daily with a gentle cleanser
- Avoid touching the face
- Use non-comedogenic oil-free moisturisers and makeup
- Remove makeup before sleeping
- Stay hydrated and maintain a balanced diet
- Manage stress levels
