DISEASE: Sun and Sunlight Damage
ALSO KNOWN AS: Photoaging, Sunburn, Solar Damage, UV Damage

OVERVIEW:
Sun damage refers to skin changes caused by prolonged or repeated exposure to ultraviolet radiation. It includes sunburn (acute) and photoaging (chronic), and increases skin cancer risk.

CAUSES:
- UVA rays: penetrate deep, cause aging and wrinkles
- UVB rays: affect outer skin layer, cause sunburn
- Cumulative lifetime sun exposure
- Tanning beds emit both UVA and UVB
- Certain medications increase photosensitivity

SYMPTOMS — ACUTE (SUNBURN):
- Red, painful, hot skin
- Swelling and blisters in severe burns
- Peeling skin as burn heals
- Headache, fever, nausea in severe cases (sun poisoning)

SYMPTOMS — CHRONIC (PHOTOAGING):
- Wrinkles and fine lines
- Dark spots or age spots (solar lentigines)
- Rough, leathery, or uneven skin texture
- Broken blood vessels on face (telangiectasia)
- Loss of skin elasticity
- Actinic keratoses (precancerous patches)

TREATMENT:
- Sunburn: cool water, aloe vera, ibuprofen, moisturiser
- Photoaging: retinoids, chemical peels, laser resurfacing, IPL treatment
- Vitamin C serum and antioxidants improve skin quality
- Actinic keratoses require medical treatment

IS IT CONTAGIOUS: No.

WHEN TO SEE A DOCTOR:
- Severe sunburn with blistering, fever, or confusion
- Dark spots that change in appearance
- Any new or changing skin lesion after sun exposure

PREVENTION:
- Apply broad-spectrum SPF 50+ sunscreen every day
- Reapply every 2 hours when outdoors
- Wear UV-protective clothing, hats, and sunglasses
- Seek shade between 10am and 4pm
- Never use tanning beds
