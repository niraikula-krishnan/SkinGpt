DISEASE: Infestations and Bites
ALSO KNOWN AS: Scabies, Lice, Insect Bites, Parasite Infections

OVERVIEW:
Skin infestations occur when parasites such as mites, lice, or insects invade the skin and cause irritation, itching, and rashes. Common types include scabies, head lice, and insect bites from mosquitoes, fleas, and bedbugs.

CAUSES:
- Scabies: Sarcoptes scabiei mite burrows into skin
- Head lice: Pediculus humanus capitis spreads by direct head contact
- Insect bites: mosquitoes, fleas, bedbugs, ticks
- Close contact with infected individuals or contaminated bedding

SYMPTOMS:
- Intense itching especially at night (scabies)
- Thin irregular burrow lines on skin (scabies)
- Small red bumps, blisters, or hives at bite sites
- Itchy scalp, visible nits (lice eggs) on hair (head lice)
- Red, swollen, irritated skin around bite
- Secondary bacterial infection from scratching

TREATMENT:
- Scabies: permethrin cream 5% applied to whole body, oral ivermectin for severe cases
- Head lice: permethrin or malathion shampoo, fine-tooth comb to remove nits
- Insect bites: antihistamines, hydrocortisone cream, cold compress
- Antibiotics if secondary infection develops
- Wash all bedding, clothing in hot water

IS IT CONTAGIOUS: Yes — scabies and lice spread through direct contact.

WHEN TO SEE A DOCTOR:
- Intense itching not relieved by OTC treatments
- Signs of secondary skin infection
- Widespread rash or fever following bites

PREVENTION:
- Avoid close contact with infected individuals
- Do not share combs, hats, bedding
- Use insect repellent containing DEET
- Inspect skin after outdoor activities in wooded areas
