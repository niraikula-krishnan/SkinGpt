DISEASE: Eczema
ALSO KNOWN AS: Atopic Dermatitis, Atopic Eczema

OVERVIEW:
Eczema is a chronic inflammatory skin condition causing itchy, inflamed, and irritated skin. It often begins in childhood and can persist into adulthood. It is closely linked to asthma and hay fever (atopic triad).

CAUSES:
- Genetic mutations affecting skin barrier function
- Immune system dysfunction causing overreaction to triggers
- Environmental triggers: dust mites, pet dander, pollen, mold
- Skin irritants: soaps, detergents, fragrances, wool
- Stress worsens flares
- Climate extremes: dry cold air or heat and sweating
- Food allergies in some children

SYMPTOMS:
- Dry, sensitive skin
- Intense itching especially at night
- Red to brownish-gray patches
- Small raised bumps that may weep fluid when scratched
- Thickened, cracked, or scaly skin
- Raw, swollen skin from scratching
- Most commonly affects hands, feet, ankles, wrists, neck, upper chest, eyelids, inside elbows and knees

TREATMENT:
- Moisturisers applied frequently to maintain skin barrier
- Topical corticosteroids to reduce inflammation during flares
- Topical calcineurin inhibitors: tacrolimus, pimecrolimus as steroid alternatives
- Dupilumab (Dupixent): biologic injection for moderate-severe eczema
- Antihistamines for itching and sleep
- Wet wrap therapy for severe flares
- Identify and avoid personal triggers

IS IT CONTAGIOUS: No.

WHEN TO SEE A DOCTOR:
- Eczema affecting daily life or sleep
- Signs of skin infection: increased redness, warmth, pus
- Symptoms not controlled with moisturisers and OTC treatments

PREVENTION:
- Moisturise skin at least twice daily
- Use gentle fragrance-free products
- Wear soft breathable cotton clothing
- Identify and avoid personal triggers
- Keep fingernails short to reduce scratching damage
