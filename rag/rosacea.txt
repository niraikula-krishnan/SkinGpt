DISEASE: Rosacea

OVERVIEW:
Rosacea is a chronic skin condition causing redness, visible blood vessels, and sometimes acne-like bumps on the face. It commonly affects middle-aged women with fair skin but can affect anyone.

CAUSES:
- Exact cause unknown
- Combination of hereditary and environmental factors
- Triggers: sun exposure, hot drinks, spicy foods, alcohol, temperature extremes, exercise, stress, certain skincare products
- Demodex mite overgrowth on skin
- Helicobacter pylori bacteria may play a role

SYMPTOMS:
- Facial redness especially on cheeks, nose, forehead, chin
- Visible small blood vessels (telangiectasia)
- Red, swollen bumps resembling acne
- Eye irritation (ocular rosacea): red, watery, irritated eyes
- Enlarged nose from thickened skin (rhinophyma) in severe cases
- Burning or stinging sensation on face

TYPES:
- Erythematotelangiectatic: redness and visible blood vessels
- Papulopustular: redness with acne-like breakouts
- Phymatous: skin thickening especially on nose
- Ocular: eye symptoms

TREATMENT:
- Topical: metronidazole, azelaic acid, ivermectin cream
- Oral antibiotics: doxycycline for bumps and pimples
- Laser therapy for visible blood vessels and redness
- Brimonidine gel to temporarily reduce redness
- Avoid known triggers

IS IT CONTAGIOUS: No.

WHEN TO SEE A DOCTOR:
- Persistent facial redness not explained by other causes
- Eye irritation with facial redness
- Skin thickening on nose

PREVENTION:
- Identify and avoid personal triggers
- Use broad-spectrum SPF 30+ sunscreen daily
- Use gentle fragrance-free skincare products
