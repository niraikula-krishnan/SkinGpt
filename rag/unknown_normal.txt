DISEASE: Unknown or Normal Skin
ALSO KNOWN AS: Normal Skin, Unidentified Skin Condition

OVERVIEW:
Sometimes a skin image may not clearly match a known disease pattern, or the skin may appear completely normal. A normal skin finding means no active disease is detected.

WHAT NORMAL SKIN LOOKS LIKE:
- Even skin tone without persistent redness or patches
- Smooth texture without unusual growths or lesions
- No blisters, sores, or persistent rashes
- Hydrated, not excessively dry or oily

WHEN THE MODEL SAYS UNKNOWN:
- The image quality may be poor or too distant
- The skin area shown may not have enough diagnostic features
- The condition may not be in the training dataset
- Lighting or angle may affect detection

WHAT TO DO:
- Upload a clearer, well-lit, close-up photo of the affected area
- Describe your symptoms to get better guidance from the AI assistant
- If concerned about a skin condition, consult a dermatologist
- Do not rely solely on AI prediction for medical decisions

WHEN TO SEE A DOCTOR:
- Any skin change that persists more than 2-3 weeks
- Symptoms like itching, pain, bleeding, or spreading rash
- Lesions that change in size, shape, or color
- Any concern about skin health regardless of AI result
