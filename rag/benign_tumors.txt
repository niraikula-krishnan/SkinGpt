DISEASE: Benign Tumors
ALSO KNOWN AS: Noncancerous Skin Growths, Lipomas, Seborrheic Keratoses

OVERVIEW:
Benign skin tumors are noncancerous growths that do not spread to other parts of the body. Common types include lipomas (fatty lumps), seborrheic keratoses (wart-like growths), and dermatofibromas.

CAUSES:
- Aging is the main cause
- Genetics and family history
- Sun exposure for some types
- Trauma to skin for dermatofibromas

SYMPTOMS:
- Raised, round, or oval growths
- Soft (lipomas) or firm (dermatofibromas)
- Brown, black, or tan colored (seborrheic keratoses)
- Waxy, scaly, or stuck-on appearance
- Usually painless unless irritated

TREATMENT:
- Often no treatment needed unless cosmetic concern
- Cryotherapy to freeze off growths
- Surgical removal or excision
- Laser treatment for cosmetic reasons

IS IT CONTAGIOUS: No.

WHEN TO SEE A DOCTOR:
- Rapid growth or change in appearance
- Pain, bleeding, or irritation
- Concern about appearance

PREVENTION:
- Protect skin from excessive sun exposure
- Regular skin self-exams
