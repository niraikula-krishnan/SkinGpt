	DISEASE: Tinea
ALSO KNOWN AS: Ringworm, Athlete's Foot, Jock Itch, Fungal Infection

OVERVIEW:
Tinea is a group of fungal skin infections caused by dermatophytes (fungi that feed on keratin). Despite the name ringworm, no worm is involved. Named by location: tinea pedis (feet), tinea cruris (groin), tinea capitis (scalp), tinea corporis (body).

CAUSES:
- Dermatophyte fungi: Trichophyton, Microsporum, Epidermophyton
- Spreads through direct skin contact with infected person or animal
- Contact with contaminated surfaces: floors, towels, clothing
- Warm moist environments promote fungal growth

SYMPTOMS:
- Ring-shaped red scaly rash with clear center (tinea corporis)
- Itchy, burning, cracked skin between toes (athlete's foot)
- Red itchy rash in groin and inner thighs (jock itch)
- Scaly patches and hair loss on scalp (tinea capitis)
- Thickened, discolored, brittle nails (tinea unguium / onychomycosis)
- Itching and flaking

TREATMENT:
- Topical antifungals: clotrimazole, terbinafine, miconazole for 2-4 weeks
- Oral antifungals: terbinafine, griseofulvin for scalp or nail infections
- Keep affected area clean and dry
- Treatment duration is important to prevent recurrence

IS IT CONTAGIOUS: Yes — spreads through direct contact and contaminated surfaces.

WHEN TO SEE A DOCTOR:
- Rash not improving after 2 weeks of antifungal treatment
- Scalp or nail involvement
- Widespread infection or signs of bacterial secondary infection

PREVENTION:
- Keep skin clean and dry especially feet and groin
- Wear flip-flops in public showers and pools
- Do not share towels, socks, or shoes
- Change socks and underwear daily
- Treat infected pets promptly
