DISEASE: Actinic Keratosis
ALSO KNOWN AS: Solar Keratosis, Precancerous skin lesion

OVERVIEW:
Actinic keratosis is a rough scaly patch on skin caused by years of sun exposure. It is considered precancerous and can develop into squamous cell carcinoma if untreated.

CAUSES:
- Cumulative UV radiation from sun or tanning beds
- Fair skin, light hair, light eyes increase risk
- Weakened immune system
- Age over 40 — more common in older adults
- Living in sunny climates

SYMPTOMS:
- Rough, dry, scaly patch of skin, usually less than 1 inch
- Flat to slightly raised patch on the top skin layer
- Hard, wart-like surface
- Color varies: pink, red, brown
- Itching, burning, or tenderness in the affected area
- Most commonly on face, lips, ears, forearms, scalp, neck, back of hands

TREATMENT:
- Cryotherapy: freezing with liquid nitrogen (most common)
- Topical creams: fluorouracil, imiquimod, diclofenac, ingenol mebutate
- Photodynamic therapy: light-activated chemical treatment
- Curettage: scraping off the lesion
- Laser therapy for widespread lesions

IS IT CONTAGIOUS: No.

WHEN TO SEE A DOCTOR:
- Any new rough scaly patch that does not heal
- Lesion that bleeds, grows, or changes
- Multiple patches appearing

PREVENTION:
- Use SPF 30+ sunscreen daily
- Wear protective clothing and hats
- Avoid peak sun hours and tanning beds
- Annual skin checks with a dermatologist
