DISEASE: Psoriasis

OVERVIEW:
Psoriasis is a chronic autoimmune skin condition that causes skin cells to multiply up to 10 times faster than normal, resulting in scaly red patches. It is not curable but manageable.

CAUSES:
- Autoimmune dysfunction — T cells attack healthy skin cells
- Genetics: 1 in 3 people with psoriasis have a family member with it
- Triggers: stress, infections (strep throat), medications (lithium, beta blockers), skin injury, smoking, alcohol

SYMPTOMS:
- Red patches of skin covered with thick silvery scales (plaques)
- Dry, cracked skin that may bleed
- Itching, burning, or soreness
- Thickened, pitted, or ridged nails
- Swollen and stiff joints (psoriatic arthritis)
- Most common on elbows, knees, scalp, lower back

TYPES:
- Plaque psoriasis: most common type
- Guttate psoriasis: small drop-shaped lesions, often triggered by strep
- Pustular psoriasis: pus-filled blisters
- Inverse psoriasis: smooth red lesions in skin folds
- Erythrodermic psoriasis: severe, affects entire body

TREATMENT:
- Topical: corticosteroids, vitamin D analogues, retinoids, coal tar
- Phototherapy: UVB light therapy
- Oral: methotrexate, cyclosporine, acitretin
- Biologics: TNF inhibitors (adalimumab), IL-17 inhibitors (secukinumab), IL-23 inhibitors

IS IT CONTAGIOUS: No.

WHEN TO SEE A DOCTOR:
- Widespread rash covering large body areas
- Joint pain or swelling accompanying skin symptoms
- Skin not responding to over-the-counter treatments

PREVENTION:
- Manage stress
- Avoid known triggers
- Moisturise skin regularly
- Limit alcohol and quit smoking
