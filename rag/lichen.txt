DISEASE: Lichen
ALSO KNOWN AS: Lichen Planus, Lichen Sclerosus, Lichen Simplex Chronicus

OVERVIEW:
Lichen refers to a group of chronic inflammatory skin conditions. Lichen planus is the most common type, causing purple itchy flat-topped bumps on skin and lacy white patches in the mouth. It is thought to be an autoimmune condition. Lichen sclerosus affects mostly the genital and anal areas. Lichen simplex chronicus results from repetitive scratching.

CAUSES:
- Lichen planus: autoimmune reaction, exact cause unknown. Triggers include hepatitis C infection, certain medications (NSAIDs, beta blockers, antimalarials), dental materials containing mercury, stress
- Lichen sclerosus: autoimmune factors, hormonal imbalances, genetics, previous skin damage
- Lichen simplex chronicus: chronic scratching or rubbing of skin, often linked to anxiety, stress, eczema, or psoriasis
- All forms may be triggered or worsened by stress

SYMPTOMS OF LICHEN PLANUS:
- Purple or violet flat-topped shiny bumps (papules)
- Intense itching
- Lacy white streaks on the bumps called Wickham striae
- Painful sores in the mouth (oral lichen planus) — lacy white patches or red painful ulcers
- Affects inner wrists, ankles, lower back, neck, genitals
- Nail changes including thinning, ridging, or loss of nails
- Scalp involvement can cause hair loss

SYMPTOMS OF LICHEN SCLEROSUS:
- White shiny patches of skin usually on genitals
- Itching, often severe
- Skin soreness and pain
- Smooth white spots initially becoming crinkled or wrinkled
- Blistering or bleeding in severe cases
- Pain during sex or urination

SYMPTOMS OF LICHEN SIMPLEX CHRONICUS:
- Thick leathery skin (lichenification)
- Scaly patches
- Intense itching especially at night
- Raw or bleeding skin from scratching

TREATMENT:
- Corticosteroid creams and ointments to reduce inflammation and itching
- Oral corticosteroids for widespread or severe cases
- Antihistamines to reduce itching
- Retinoids (vitamin A derivatives) for stubborn cases
- Immunosuppressants like cyclosporine or methotrexate for severe lichen planus
- Phototherapy with UVB light
- Tacrolimus or pimecrolimus ointments as steroid alternatives
- Treating underlying hepatitis C if associated with lichen planus
- Psychological support and stress management for lichen simplex chronicus

IS IT CONTAGIOUS: No, lichen conditions are not contagious.

COMPLICATIONS:
- Scarring especially on scalp causing permanent hair loss
- Increased risk of squamous cell carcinoma in long-standing oral or genital lichen planus
- Lichen sclerosus in genitals can cause scarring and sexual dysfunction
- Nail damage can be permanent
- Psychological distress from chronic itching and appearance

WHEN TO SEE A DOCTOR:
- Purple itchy bumps appearing on skin or wrists
- White lacy patches or sores inside the mouth
- Genital skin changes including white patches or persistent itching
- Nail changes that cannot be explained
- Itching that disrupts sleep or daily life
- Any skin lesion that changes, bleeds, or does not heal

PREVENTION:
- No known way to fully prevent lichen conditions
- Manage stress effectively
- Avoid known drug triggers under medical supervision
- Treat hepatitis C infection if present
- Avoid repetitive scratching of itchy skin
- Keep skin moisturised with fragrance-free products
- Wear loose comfortable clothing to reduce skin irritation
